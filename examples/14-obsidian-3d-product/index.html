[ 03 / THE STORY ]

Inspired by volcanic glass.
Engineered in Geneva.

Obsidian is formed when molten lava cools so rapidly it has no time to crystallize. The result is a natural glass — black, sharp, and impossibly smooth. A stone forged in fire, finished in silence.

The Seven takes this idea as its starting point. A case milled from a single billet of titanium 904L. A dial cut from real obsidian, sourced from the mountains of Hrafntinna, Iceland. A movement hand-assembled by a single watchmaker in the Vallée de Joux.

No marketing words on the case back. No synthetic sapphire where a real stone will do. No compromises. Just a watch, built the way watches were built when a watch was the most complicated object a person could own.
